Inhabitants

   The number of inhabitants will decide the initial size of the World

Land Plots

  

Land Plots are calculated by the Total Inhabitants /a random number between 10 to 40.
Average Inhabitants
on Land Plots		   Some Land Plots will have more and some Land Plots will have less inhabitants, based on a random generator between 10 and 100.
The naturalized number of inhabitants on each plot of Land will be on a ratio of 40:1
The maximum number of inhabitants on each plot of Land will be on a ratio of 100:1
The minimum number of inhabitants on each plot cannot be less than on a ratio fo 10:1

Tribes

  

The inhabitants will be Grouped into Tribes and divided into Territories. 
The Tribes will be determined by the Land Plots with the most inhabitants above the 40:1 naturalization value (which is the value which supports Civilization and Tribalism). Any value below 40:1 is Barbarianism or Serfdom to Tribes.
Some Territories will remain blank.

Territory

  

Once the number of Tribes are determined, a formula can divide the Land Plots equally between the Tribes.
Proximity can also determine which Land Plots belong to which Tribes. A formula to group all Land Plots within the closest Proximity to a Tribe can be devised.
The Tribe Proximity formula can also use link blocks to determine Tribal Affiliation. If there is an open path from a Tribe which is not obstructed by the sphere of influence of another Tribe, this falls within Proximity.

Resources

  

A formula will decide the total quantity of resources available. 
A second equation will determine the resource type
A third equation will decide the Resource location on the Map (which polygon owns the resource).
